## Key Learnings

- Supply chain optimization requires balancing cost, speed, risk, and sustainability instead of maximizing only one metric.
- Multiple suppliers improve resilience by reducing dependency on a single vendor.
- Domestic manufacturing improves quality control and delivery speed but increases production costs.
- A hybrid warehouse strategy balances operational cost with faster customer deliveries.
- Road freight offers a practical balance between speed, flexibility, and cost.
- Balanced inventory reduces stockout risk without creating excessive storage costs.
- Every supply chain decision creates trade-offs that affect overall business performance.
