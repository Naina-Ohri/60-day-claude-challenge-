## Key Learnings

- Headlines are often designed to attract attention rather than communicate facts accurately.
- Emotional language can influence how readers react before they verify information.
- Manipulation techniques such as fear, urgency, curiosity, and exaggeration are commonly used in online content.
- Comparing headlines with the actual article helps identify misleading framing.
- Thinking critically before sharing information improves media literacy.
- Evaluating sources objectively helps reduce misinformation.
