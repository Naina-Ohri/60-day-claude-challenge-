                Methodology & Data Sources

                Air quality: AQI, PM2.5 and PM10 figures use the India CPCB National AQI scale (0–500; Good/Satisfactory/Moderate/Poor/Very Poor/Severe), grounded in current public monitoring readings for Delhi and other major Indian cities as a same-day snapshot. Live AQI fluctuates hour to hour with traffic, weather and season — treat these as a representative point-in-time read, not a permanent ranking.

                Water quality: Water Quality Scores are a composite illustrative index (0–100) built from publicly known regional groundwater/water-supply patterns (e.g. high TDS & hardness in Delhi, fluoride belts in Rajasthan, arsenic-prone zones in parts of Bihar & West Bengal, Ganga-basin industrial effluent near Kanpur). This is not a live per-city sensor feed — use it directionally, not as a certified lab reading for your specific tap.

                Time-of-Day Pattern & trend chart: modelled from documented diurnal pollution behaviour (morning traffic build-up, afternoon dispersal, night-time temperature inversion) and is explicitly illustrative.

                Health impact & recommendations: general, population-level associations between pollution/water exposure and common wellbeing domains — for personal symptoms or medical decisions, please consult a qualified doctor or dermatologist.

                Personal Environmental Health Analyzer — built as a single self-contained HTML file. Works fully offline once loaded (charts/fonts need an internet connection on first load).
